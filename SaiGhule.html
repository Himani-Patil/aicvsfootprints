Is Engineering All About Getting a Fancy Job ?

By Sai Ghule

As A part of AICVS Webinar 3.0, AICVS invited Sai Ghule, Btech student at Cummins College of Engineering for women to share her experiences and journey of the four long years of Engineering and also guide the enthusiastic newbies through the same.

Courses and Curriculum

To kick off the session, Sai Ghule gave a brief talk on the importance of taking Courses alongside the day-to-day college curriculum. Discussing her own journey, she suggested a few courses including the WebDev bootcamp on Udemy, Deep learning Specialization On Coursera and also certain IBM Cognitive Courses. She shed light on how these diverse courses helped her find her actual interest and shaped her coming undergrad years.

Research

Sai further went on to talk about her role as a Lead Research fellow at UET California where she worked on Genetic Algorithms. She highlighted the importance of networking and the concept of cold emailing to get opportunities in research and cooperate internships. She also talked about her time as the Management Trainee at Emergetech Foundation where she headed her department and was responsible for planning, strategizing and recruiting. She not only gained heaps of exposure from this position but also found her real passion.

Mentorship Programs

Sai listed various mentorship programs including PwC, Citi Bridge, MS Engage and DE Shaw Ascend. She emphasized the importance of the mentorships and also narrated her experiences with the PwC and the Citi Bridge mentorship.

Following this, Sai Discussed her multiple leadership roles as the T&P representative for TY and The Operations Secretary of Cummins and also encouraged others to take up similar responsibilities.

Moving on, Sai spoke about her research project at IIT Bombay which was a part of her BTech project. She worked on ‘Generating Compiler Phrases from Specifications of Intermediate Representation’, a research project based on Core Computer science subject of compilers.

Placement

Sai concluded the session by dropping some tips for placement interview preparation highlighting the importance of coding every day, practicing DSA and core CS subjects including OOP, OS and DBMS.

Finally, Sai offered insight into some post Undergrad options such as a Masters in CS/Mech/Electronics, MBA or pursuing a research fellowship.

The session ended with an enthusiastic QnA session concluding yet another successful Webinar in the series of AICVS Webinar 3.0
